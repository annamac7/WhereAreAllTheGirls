K-8 Education

From a young age girls are exposed to marketing techniques that assocaite jobs in the tech industry with men.
There are few female role models in tech for young girls to look up to and inspire to be like.

High School

Girls repersent 56% of AP test takers, but only 19% of AP Computer Science test takers.This is not a statistic that is applicable to all STEM subjects, as girls account for 46% of the AP Calc test takers.
Additionally, enrollment in advanced math and science courses do not differ by gender. But they do differ considerably when it comes to computer sciences courses.

University Level

Women earn 57% of all undergraduate degrees, but only 18% of computer science degrees.
Some women admitted that they feel intimidated by the major since at most schools it is dominated by males. They feel that other liberal arts majors are targeted more positivly towards girls

In the Workplace

Women repersent half of the college educated workforce in the United States, but only repersent a quarter of the Computer Science workforce.
The hiring process for many computer science jobs have been reported to be biased towards males. Many companies don't have quotas for the number of women that they are required to hire.
There is also a huge gender wage gap that discourages women from applying to tech jobs.
