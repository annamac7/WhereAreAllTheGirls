K-8 Education

Introducing mentorship programs will give young girls female role models in tech to aspire to be like. This will also help spark their interest early, making them more likely to stick with computer science activities as they profress through their education. This will also help remove the negative connotations that young girls might have about the tech industry.

High School

Encourage high school girls to create / get involved in "girls in tech" clubs and afterschool activies. There are many of these clubs geared towards boys, but not many for girls. Introducing these programs would even out the playing field of tech-centered opprotunities for boys and girls.

University Level

Have universities and computer science departments encourage women to get involved by offer opprotunities that would allow women to learn elementary coding skills in a non-intimidating, non-biased environment.
Hold workshops in computer science departments that work on getting rid of sexism in the field.

In the Workplace

Encourage companies to introduce quotas that encourage the hiring of females to help even out the gender gap in the industry.
Introduce workshops in the workplace to teach employees about sexism and bias in the field.
Hold "women in tech" confrences where women can meet other women in the field.
Reduce the gender wage gap.
